I originally studied Mechanical Engineering with Advanced Design and Innovation at the University of Bath. While there, I found that my focus changed. Instead of designing mechanical parts and systems, I found much greater satisfaction when designing for people - talking to them, discovering their needs, coming up with solutions, and finding what worked (and much more importantly, what didn't)!

Becuase of this, my last two years at Bath were a bit more unconventional. While others designed cars, planes and other physical products, I found myself looking into more subjective areas, like;

  • the inspiration behind design,
  • what causes design to change,
  • the effects of these changes,
  • idea generation through non-standard techniques,

before using my dissertation to look into ways of optimising design, such as;

  • creating rules for concept development,
  • studying the language used in brainstorming/concept development sessions,
  • ways to increase idea output and boost creativity.

What I realised during this was that I was moving away from traditional engineering design (based around machines and systems) and instead closer to newer fields such as UX design more than ever before.

And more importantly, I really liked it.

So, after graduating, I took some time out to delve deeper into user experience design, touching on areas such as persuasive design, behavioural design and information architecture, as well as learning front end development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) and some of the things that make it quicker (Bootstrap, SASS, jQuery).

But anyway, enough of me talking. Take a look around my site (or poke around the source if you're so inclined), and see what I've actually done!

And if you like what you see, let's talk!
